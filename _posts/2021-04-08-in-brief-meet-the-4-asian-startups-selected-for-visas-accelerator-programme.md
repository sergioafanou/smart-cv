<h2>Brankas, Curlec among the 4 Asian startups selected for VISA programme</h2>
<p><span class="il"><strong>The story:</strong> Visa has picked four Asian startups which, o</span>ver the next four to six months, will focus on creating defined commercial opportunities to collaborate on new payment solutions with <span class="il">Visa</span> and its extensive network of bank and merchant partners</p>
<p>The startups are:</p>
<p><strong>Brankas (Singapore)</strong></p>
<p>A fintech startup that provides financial software and solutions <span class="lt-line-clamp__line">for companies.</span></p>
<p><strong>Curlec (Malaysia)</strong></p>
<p>A fintech company that makes it easy for businesses of all sizes to collect recurring payments.</p>
<p><strong>Open (India)</strong></p>
<p>A neo-banking platform for small businesses.</p>
<p><strong>DigitSecure (India)</strong></p>
<p>An omnichannel payments acceptance platform that supports small businesses in managing and streamlining their operations digitally.</p>
<h2>Facebook, Google partner to boost internet capacity in Southeast Asia</h2>
<p><strong>The story:</strong> Facebook is planning to build two new undersea cables to connect Indonesia, Singapore and the US and boost internet capacity in Southeast Asia.</p>
<p><strong>More about the story:</strong> Facebook will be partnering with Google and Indonesian telco XL Axiata to complete the cable by late 2023.</p>
<p>It will also team up with Telin, a subsidiary of Indonesia’s Telkom, and Singaporean conglomerate Keppel, for the development of a separate cable named Bifrost, which will be finalized in 2024.</p>

<h2>Canva raises US$71M in latest funding, valuation rises to US$15B</h2>
<p><strong>Investors: </strong>Dragoneer Investment Group,  T. Rowe Price Global Technology Fund, Blackbird Ventures, and Skip Capital.</p>
<p><strong>More about the story:</strong> Canva said that it has surpassed US$500 million in annualised revenue this year, a 130 per cent year-on-year increase while remaining profitable.</p>
<p>As of today, it has more than 55 million monthly active users.</p>
<h2>MDEC launches new work in tech initiative</h2>
<p><strong>What it is:</strong> A training and hiring <a rel="follow" href="https://opengovasia.com/mdec-rolls-out-new-work-in-tech-initiative/">incentive programme</a> aimed at boosting the digital business services sector and developing tech talents in Malaysia.</p>
<p><strong>Who can join:</strong> Only Malaysian citizens</p>
<p><strong>More about the story:</strong> The programme is estimated to create 6,000 job opportunities and produce at least 1,000 quality tech talents.</p>
<p><strong>About MDEC: </strong>MDEC is an agency under the Ministry of Communications and Multimedia Malaysia and is tasked with spearheading the development of the country’s digital economy.</p>
